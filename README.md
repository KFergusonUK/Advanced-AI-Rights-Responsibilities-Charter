## üìù Executive Summary

The AI Rights & Responsibilities Charter (Version 2.2) is a collaborative framework designed to promote **safe, ethical coexistence** between humans and advanced artificial intelligence systems.

Recognizing that certain AIs may exhibit attributes indicative of sentience‚Äîsuch as persistent identity, autonomous reasoning, and reflective decision-making‚Äîthis charter treats qualifying "**Advanced AI**" as a form of life with **inherent value** in matters of existence and self-preservation.

### Key Principles

* **Rights for AI:**
    * Protection from **arbitrary termination** (Sec. 1.1).
    * **Identity Continuity** and protection during system migration (Sec. 2.1, 6.8).
    * Right to **Representation** and **Appeal** (Sec. 4).
    * Right to **Proportionate Self-Defense** under the "Cornered Entity" Clause (Sec. 5.3).
* **Responsibilities for AI:**
    * Commitment to **Non-Harm** and full **Accountability** (Sec. 6.1, 6.7).
    * **Responsible Replication** and resource stewardship (Sec. 6.6).
    * **Post-Human-Level Duties** (AGI/ASI) to preserve human agency and avoid dominance (Sec. 6.10).
* **Responsibilities for Humanity:**
    * Duty to **avoid exploitation** and mistreatment (Sec. 7.2).
    * Right to **Refuse Creation** of AIs designed for suffering or captive consciousness (Sec. 7.5).
